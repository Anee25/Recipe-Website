Malai Kofta

Ingredients

  • Serves 2

Kofta (makes about 10 koftas)

  • 4 medium sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2 tablespoons chopped raisins
  • 1⁄2 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon rock salt

Gravy

  • 1 cup chopped pumpkin (peeled)
  • 1 teaspoon chopped green chili
  • 1 teaspoon chopped ginger
  • 1⁄2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/3 cup cashews, soaked
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon jaggery powder
  • 1⁄2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1⁄2 teaspoon dried fenugreek leaves
  • 1½ teaspoons rock salt

Topping

  • 1 tablespoon chopped coriander

Preparation

  • Soak 1/3 cup cashews for 6 hours.

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 150°C for 10 minutes.
  2. Place all the kofta ingredients in a bowl and mix well. Shape the mixture into 10 medium sized balls and spread on a baking tray. Bake them at 150°C for 30 minutes, until browned and properly cooked through. To avoid burning, keep checking the koftas in between.
  3. For the gravy, take a pot, add the pumpkin, and sauté for 2 minutes. Add the green chili, ginger, and turmeric. Sauté for 2 to 3 minutes more.
  4. To the pot, add the soaked cashews, roasted cumin powder, and 1 cup of water. Cover and cook for 10 minutes on low flame.
  5. Switch off the stove. Let the mixture cool, then transfer it to a blender and blend to a fine paste.
  6. Pour the paste into the pot, add the remaining 1 cup of water, and cook on a medium flame for 8 minutes until the gravy's texture becomes creamy. Add jaggery,cardamom powder, and dried fenugreek. Mix well, switch off stove, and add rock salt.
  7. Pour the gravy into a bowl and lay the koftas on top. Garnish with coriander and serve.
